# Skill 01: Body Relaxation

Cultivation: 01 - Mindfulness of Body
Subtitle: How to transform physical restlessness into body relaxation
Duration: Initially 20 minutes, gradually increasing to 30 minutes

> "A meditator, having gone to the wilderness, to the shade of a tree, or a secluded place, sits down, folds their legs crosswise, holds their body erect and arouses mindfulness in the forefront of their mind." â€” The Buddha, MN 119

## Overview

Your next step is to increase your body awareness by enjoying how nice it feels to relax your body with slow, gentle breathing. This step helps to alleviate feelings of physical restlessness related to stress and enhances body awareness in preparation for mindfulness of breathing in Skill 05.

## Benefits

- This Meditation Skill will teach you how to lower your experience of physical restlessness and access the first stage of meditation, feeling comfortable in your body.

## Purpose

To teach your mind to find enjoyment in relaxing your body and to settle stress-based restlessness as preparation for mindfulness of your body.

## Instructions

### Step 1: Preparation for meditation

In this meditation, you move from lying down in Skill 00 to sitting upright with your legs crossed on a cushion on the floor, on a kneeling stool, or resting on a comfortable, supportive chair.

Sit in meditation with one palm resting in the other, and feel the awareness of your whole body as you sit in meditation. You will be using awareness of your body as the meditation object.

### Step 2: Develop mindfulness

Mindfulness is the meditative skill of gently remembering (keeping in mind) what you are experiencing now in your body, mind and senses.

Listen to the flow of sound: Actively listen to sounds around you, the way they flow and change. Allow the presence of sound to develop mindfulness by gently keeping it in mind.

Feel your clothing on your body: As you relax, you will naturally become aware of your clothing touching your body and the coolness/warmth of air touching your skin.

### Step 3: Relax your body with slow breaths

In MIDL, these slow, relaxing breaths are known as softening breaths because your body and mind become softer as you relax.

Relax with diaphragmatic (belly) breaths: Take five softening breaths by slowly moving your belly outward and then slowly lowering it back.

Relax with full-body breaths: Take five more slow inhales, starting in your belly, bringing the breath up to fill your chest, and then, with each exhale, allow your whole body to relax.

### Step 4: Enjoy the relaxation

After taking these softening breaths, allow your breathing to return to its natural rhythm; enjoy the growing relaxation and comfort in your body for the rest of the meditation.

## Tips

- Tune into how nice it feels to be present with these simple experiences: this is the key to deepening meditation.

## Obstacles

### Physical Restlessness

Unable to experience physical comfort. Physical Restlessness refers to feeling of being unsettled in your body, which prompts you to move around and fidget. It occurs during meditation due to the build-up of energy from stress, aversion, or overstimulation in our daily lives.

## Insight

- Marker: 01 - Body Relaxation
- Hindrance: 01 - Physical Restlessness
- Antidote: Developing your skill in relaxing your body creates the conditions for Marker 01. To settle physical restlessness, cultivate curiosity in finding enjoyment as you relax your body with slow, diaphragmatic, belly breaths. As you exhale, allow a part of your body, such as your shoulders, to relax slowly and deliberately so that you can feel the bodily relaxation.

## Daily Application

Check-in: "Are you holding too much effort in your body?"

### Learnings

- To recognise physical restlessness.
- To relax physical restlessness.
- To appreciate how nice it feels to relax your body.
- To notice that whenever you relax your body, awareness of your body increases.

### Steps

1. Enjoy noticing when you've forgotten to be mindful.
2. Take a few breaths to relax your body when you notice.
3. Finding enjoyment in the relaxing breaths.
4. Clearly comprehending awareness returning to your body as you do.
5. Being satisfied with creating small gaps in mind wandering.

## Progression

Criteria: You are ready to progress when you experience periods of feeling relaxed and comfortable during your meditation, and you understand how to settle physical restlessness with slow, softening belly breaths.
